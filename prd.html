01 · Problem Statement
The Game Doesn't Celebrate Its Players

When players complete a milestone — a chapter, a quest, a level up — the game barely acknowledges what just happened. A generic popup appears with a currency drop and a button. It could be any game, any milestone, any character. There is no emotional payoff, no sense that what was just created mattered, and nothing that pulls them toward doing it again.

The game is asking players to be authors — but it isn't treating their work like it means anything.

Scope note: This PRD focuses on chapter completion as Phase 1. Chapter completion is the key creative milestone for our target audience — it is the moment a player finishes telling a piece of their story. The same thinking will need to be applied to other milestones in subsequent phases, but the design and mechanics should be validated here first.

02 · The Insight
Three Problems, One Feature

Celebration solves three problems simultaneously:

No emotional payoff after completing a chapter — the current screen is generic and forgettable · Traits have no perceived value — players assign traits but never feel them expressed or reflected back · No pull to keep players writing — there is nothing at the end of a chapter that makes a player want to continue

If the celebration is rooted in the character's voice and the player's own words — and ends with a cliffhanger that teases what's next — it stops being a UI event and becomes a story moment. Players feel like authors, not users completing a checklist.

03 · How It Works
The Sequence

The sequence replaces the current chapter completion popup entirely. One gesture — the player is the author putting the final mark on their chapter, and then the story whispers what comes next.

1
Chapter ends
2
Character's voice appears
A single AI-generated line appears on screen — written in the character's voice, about what just happened in the chapter. This is the emotional beat. The story acknowledging itself.
3
The last sentence is written
The last sentence of the chapter is written across the screen in real time — letter by letter, fast, like a quill finishing with purpose. The player watches their own words get inscribed.
4
The period appears
The sentence ends. A glowing period appears, waiting.
5
Player taps — ink blooms
The player taps the period. It lands and settles — a small, natural ink bloom spreads outward from that dot, the way ink behaves when a quill presses firmly on paper.
6
Grab Rewards
Rewards appear on screen with a "Grab Rewards" button. Tapping sends them flying up into the currency counters at the top of the screen.
7
Cliffhanger appears
One AI-generated teaser for what's coming in the next chapter — not a summary of what just happened, but a hint of what's next. The hook that keeps players writing. Two buttons: Start Next Chapter or Close.
8
Direction picker
Start Next Chapter opens a direction picker — three story paths to choose from, plus a "Generate more" option if none land.
04 · The Trait Connection
Traits Made Visible

The AI-generated line in step 2 is filtered through the character's traits. Same chapter event, different voice depending on who the character is. Players start noticing that how their story gets told back to them reflects the character they built — traits become visible without any additional UI or explanation.

Enthusiastic
"I can't believe that worked — but it did, and now everything changes."
Guarded
"I said what needed to be said. Whether it was the right call — that's another question."
Knowledgeable
"The pieces were always there. I simply arranged them."
05 · User Story
As a player...

"As a player who just finished a chapter, I want my own words and my character's voice to be part of the completion moment — so that I feel the emotional payoff of what I just created, and I'm hooked enough to keep writing."

06 · Success Metrics
How We Know It's Working
Type Metric
Primary D7 retention +5% vs control group
Primary Chapters completed per user per week +10%
Primary Session length — players continuing directly into the next chapter
Secondary Session frequency — how often players open the app per week
Guardrail Chapter completion rate — no decrease from baseline
07 · Scope
What We're Building
Phase 1 Chapter Completion
  • Chapter completion trigger only
  • AI-generated character line (one sentence, trait-informed, based on chapter content)
  • Ink writing animation of the last chapter sentence — fast, under 2 seconds
  • Glowing period interaction
  • Subtle ink bloom on tap
  • Rewards appear with "Grab Rewards" button — tapping sends rewards flying into currency counters
  • AI-generated cliffhanger line with Start Next Chapter or Close buttons
  • Start Next Chapter leads to a direction picker — three story paths plus "Generate more"
  • Close returns the player to their world
  • The player can dismiss the sequence at any point by tapping outside
Phase 2 Other Milestones

Each trigger will be designed separately, sharing the same design philosophy — celebrations rooted in the creative act, not generic game UI.

  • Quest completion (item/building/storage placed in world)
  • Character creation
  • World creation
  • Level up
08 · Risks & Mitigation
What Could Go Wrong
AI-generated lines feel generic or break character voice
Quality of the one-sentence line is the emotional core of the feature. QA first 500 generated lines before launch. Build a feedback mechanism. Set a kill threshold if quality drops below acceptable baseline.
The sequence feels slow
The ink writing animation must feel like a flourish, not a loading screen. Target under 2 seconds for the full writing sequence. Player can dismiss by tapping outside at any point.
The chapter's last sentence is too long to display well
The game may need to truncate or select a portion of the last sentence for display. Define a character limit and a fallback for edge cases before engineering begins.
Players dismiss every time
Dismissal is always available — that's intentional and correct. If the majority of players dismiss consistently, the issue is pacing or quality, not the concept. Investigate before removing the feature.
